UI refresh — softer, layered elevation

The sidebar's drop shadow reads harsh because it is a single hard-edged layer (--e-2) sized for a small popover but cast by a tall full-height panel. This proposal keeps every colour, type, spacing, radius, and layout decision exactly as-is and changes only the quality of the shadow: two-layer key+ambient shadows with low alpha and negative spread, plus a dedicated barely-there lift for large floating panels. Left = today, Right = proposed.

1 · The floating sidebar

The headline change. Today the panel sits under a uniform grey smudge; the proposal lets the hairline border do the separating and reduces the shadow to a soft ambient lift — the modern app-shell look, without moving a single element.

2 · The elevation ramp

Every shadow in the system, top to bottom. The proposed values are two layers — a tight 1–4 px key shadow that grounds the element plus a wide, low-alpha ambient that diffuses. Negative spread keeps the ambient from spilling into a grey rectangle.
